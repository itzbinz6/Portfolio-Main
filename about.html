About Me

Builder.
Student. In progress.

I'm Ajayi Abisola Enoch — a Computer Science student at Landmark University in Nigeria, and someone who has never been able to do just one thing at a time.

Web design happened naturally. I was studying CS, started building things, and realized I couldn't stop. Not because someone told me to — but because there's something satisfying about taking an idea and making it exist on a screen that anyone in the world can open. It made sense with what I was already learning, and it turned out I was good at it.

Most of my projects started the same way: I'd find a business doing real work — a restaurant, a cake studio, a café — with nothing but a WhatsApp number and an Instagram page to show for it. So I'd build them something. No brief, no commission. Just a demo and a message saying "I think you deserve better than this."

Alongside the design work, I'm also building out affiliate marketing on the side — different game, same instinct: find something genuinely useful, learn it properly, then figure out how to put it in front of the right people.

None of this happens in a vacuum either. I'm a Christian, and honestly, God has been my backing through all of it — the quiet right hand in the background when I'm stuck on something at 2am or a project doesn't go the way I planned. I don't lead with that, but it's part of how I move through all of this.

Outside of that, I'm quietly working on a few other things I'm not ready to talk about publicly yet. They're early. They're mine. And they remind me that web design is just one piece of something bigger I'm still figuring out.

When I'm not building, you'll usually find me deep in an anime that actually makes me think — Attack on Titan, Summertime Rendering, that kind of thing — or with gospel music on, Dunsin Oyekan on repeat, depending on the kind of day it's been. I also mess around with trading strategies sometimes, mostly because I like figuring out how systems work — which is probably the same itch that got me into code in the first place.

I'm building mostly alone, learning constantly, and okay with not having everything figured out yet. If something on this site made you curious — about the work, about me, about anything — my contact is right below.
